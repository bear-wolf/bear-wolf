Dr. Wells’ next consultation with Wendy took place at her office.
The doctor had asked Cheryl to join them. Wendy’s workspace,
though small, was impeccably organized and neat. Documents
and reports claimed their precise places in systematically labeled
shelves and file cabinets. The order and efficiency of her office
did not surprise the doctor in the slightest. It was clear that
Wendy lived by her philosophy of working with economy
and attention to detail.

Dr. Wells had been very tempted to analyze the results of
the questionnaire and focus groups on his own, but he had
promised Wendy they would do it together, and forwarded
her the data files. Wendy felt that analyzing the data as a
team would help keep everyone open to all possibilities.

“We’re almost there,” said Wendy, as she entered the room.
“We’ve gathered information from reliable sources. Now let’s
take a look at our results. It’s very nice to meet you, Cheryl.
Thanks so much for taking the time to come here today.”

“Thank you for everything,” said Cheryl. “Dr. Wells has been
raving about your critical thinking skills. I’m grateful to be
a part of this process,” Cheryl said.

“Why, thank you, Cheryl,” Wendy replied.

Wendy guided them toward a small conference table
where a computer screen displayed a series of charts.

“Now, let’s get right down to it, guys!”
