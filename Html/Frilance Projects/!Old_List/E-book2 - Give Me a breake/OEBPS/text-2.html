Trouble Looms in Dr. Wells’ Office

“I’ll bet this is why four billing clerks quit!” exclaimed Cheryl,
waving the prior authorization form in front of Dr. Wells. “Do you
realize what has to be done every time you prescribe a medication
the patient’s insurance company doesn’t cover? Just look at the
first page of this insane document. And, every insurance company
has a different form. Our staff wants to feel like they are part of a
team, not like a bunch of data-entry drones!”

Dr. Wells was taken aback by his head nurse’s intensity. “Cheryl,
I’m not sure I agree with you. I trust your judgment, but four
employees resigning in two weeks because of an insurance form?

I find that hard to believe.”

Cheryl continued her tirade, “Even though none of them
gave a specific reason for quitting, my money is on the prior
authorization process. They were fed up. It’s that simple!”

“There has to be more to it than that,” said Dr. Wells. “The
physician assistants, nurses, and even receptionists seem to
be down in the dumps. Could this one task really be creating
a domino effect that’s causing things to spiral out of control
in our office?”
