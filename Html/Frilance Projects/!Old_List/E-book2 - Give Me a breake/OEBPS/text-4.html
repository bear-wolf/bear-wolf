Help is On the Way!

As Wendy Berman strode through the door and extended
her hand, Dr. Wells knew immediately there would be no
uncomfortable power dynamic in this relationship. Wendy
had a commanding presence, a firm handshake, and a
friendly smile that soon put him at ease.

“Thank you for coming in, Wendy,” Dr. Wells said, as he
gestured for her to take a seat in the armchair in front
of his desk. Wendy wasted no time.

“It’s my pleasure, Dr. Wells. I’m
here to get a general sense of
the situation, and I’m all about
efficiency. So, tell me, in under
30 seconds, what’s going on,
leaving out any extraneous
information, even if you
have a strong desire to
give me details.”

“It’s pretty simple,” said
Dr. Wells. “Four of my billing
employees recently resigned
without telling me why, and everyone
in the office seems to have lost their
enthusiasm and willingness to help.
My head nurse, Cheryl, thinks it has to
do with the prior authorization form.”

“Okay, stop right there, Dr. Wells,” Wendy interjected. “Sorry
to interrupt, but remember, I need to hear only the objective
facts, not what you or anyone else believes or thinks.”

“We are trying to be objective. We have already conducted two
focus groups, handed out a job satisfaction questionnaire
to our staff, and even hired my friend’s son, who’s
home from college for the summer, to do
some research on job satisfaction in small
organizations like ours,” said Dr. Wells.

“Wow!” Wendy seemed genuinely
impressed. “You’ve done a great job
structuring a root cause analysis by
triangulating information from three
different sources.”

“I have?” asked Dr. Wells, surprised.

“Gathering information from three different
sources will help us come to a more accurate
conclusion – as long as the sources are reliable.”

“That’s the issue Cheryl and I have been arguing about. Cheryl
is already convinced she knows the cause of our problem,
but I don’t want to rely solely on the opinions of the staff.
Especially since the more outgoing employees tended to
dominate in the focus groups we conducted. Still, Cheryl has
been with me for 12 years, and she’s closer to the staff than I am.”
